Editorial

"The purpose of a writer is to keep civilization from destroying itself,"- Albert Camus Since the beginning of the human civilization, we, the humans have always been creative creatures. One of the earliest expressions of those creative works can be traced back to Literature. Literature began to truly flourish with the advent of paper, as well as the progress of civilization. As Many Oliver reminds us, that life is' wild and precious, fleeting and worthy of awe; Paper served as the tool that humanity longed and our emotions, our life journey began to be immortalised. With every stroke of the Ink on paper. we learned to place our chaos into sentences and Call it meaningful. As time went on, and civilization evolved further. Literature didn't resumed to be expression of mere emotions, it evolved into something insightful, something visionary, and Newspaper was forged. Through the insights, it began to unfold, Newspapers became the daily diary of mankind and Literature its memoir. We can take a look on "The Spectator; as an example which was a weekly British political and cultural news magazine. This historical 18th century publication by Joseph Addison and Richard Steele challenged the then existing conservative thoughts, and revolutionized the social and cultural arena Taking this vibrant example in heart, we aspire to confess through Ink and to evolve through insight.

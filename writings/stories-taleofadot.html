Tale of a dot

- Cadet Shreyan (3057)

The butterfly effect. They say a single dandelion might prevent catastrophic floods. The butterfly effect. One significant action, one fleeting thought-either can reshape the course of history. Consider a dot. A dot misplaced can transform you into a wealthy man or, well, remain nothing more than a meaningless speck. This is the tale of an unremarkable boy and a dot. A simple crimson dot that ignited a civil war. Ali Ariyan was an unremarkable fifteen-year-old boy. If anyone attempted to chronicle his life, it would be laughably brief: perhaps a few sentences devoted to his unremarkable days as a cricket fielder for the neighbourhood team. But Ariyan's existence isn't nearly as mundane as appearances suggest. His life divides into two distinct epochs: the period he played mediocre cricket and the era he became the symbol of national upheaval. All triggered by a dot. A single vivid crimson circle of bargain paint he chose to leave on a stone duelling. Everything began during a bus journey to a hill station in the North - a school excursion he'd spent all summer saving for. His excitement evaporated when they were abandoned roadside after the bus wheezed its final breath before surrendering to the scorching heat wave. Ariyan surveyed his environment: several stone dwellings and a deteriorating shop nearby. He approached the shop for water. A pot-bellied shopkeeper sat behind the grimy counter and handed over a paint brush plus a dusty palette of crimson paints instead of proper change. Glowering at the shopkeeper for this unfair exchange, Ariyan balanced the brush a top the palette and moved back towards his group. The bus engine still refused to cooperate. Bored and friendless, Ariyan returned to observing the surrounding He wandered toward one stone structure. His gaze dropped to his paint palette. The wall behind him appeared willingly to serve as canvas, so he began working, meticulously mixing the remaining water droplets into the most vibrant crimson shade - a deep blood like hue. Eventually, a bright crimson dot marked on the surface. The watery pigment trickled down to Ariyan's wrists as he reached for the bottle again. "Need more dots for balloon bouquets. Maybe a down gripping the strings?" Before completing his first balloon, his PT teacher's whistle shrieked through the atmosphere, startling him. He abandoned his art supplies and sprinted toward the bus The revived vehicle travelled deeper into the mountains, away from the heat. Months dragged on as the nation was torn into sides, the wind turning frigid and the scent of fresh blood almost detectable.
